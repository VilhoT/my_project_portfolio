Overview

The system I have built controls the garden lights with MOSFETs and relays. I control the lights via Homeassistant or use a physical switch. Right now I make my records such as lighttime, watering and temperature on paper, but this too, could be easily automated (once I find the time and will)

It works fine, but there’s a lot of room for improvement. In the future, I want to transition to a fully hydroponic system - no soil, just water with nutrients. This would maximize space efficiency and allow for better control.

Thoughts and improvement

The final system could include:

  • Pumps & solenoid valves to automate water & nutrient flow.
  • Plumbing system integration so the only manual task would be refilling the nutrient tank.
  • Advanced monitoring: pH, temperature, humidity, pressure, and water flow.
  • PAR sensor (measures the Photosynthetic Active Radiation—different from LUX).
  • Camera recognition to track growth and fruit-bearing stages.

And of course, fail-safes. The system should:

  • Detect and prevent failures.
  • Monitor weak points in real time.
  • If failure occurs, automatically control the damage.

But that’s all for the future -once I’m no longer living in a rental, my girlfriend trusts my builds enough to tap into the plumbing, and the garden isn’t in our bedroom.
