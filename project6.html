Lift Ordering Automation

Current Status

This project is still ongoing, and while I have some progress, it has proven to be much more challenging than I initially expected. The program seems to have a mind of its own, operating unpredictably. Some days it works flawlessly, while on other days it refuses to cooperate. There doesn't seem to be any common pattern or obvious factor behind its behavior.

Project Concept

The idea is to automate the process of ordering a lift when I leave home. Essentially, the program should recognize when I am about to leave and then order a lift automatically. However, the results have been quite erratic. At times, I feel like I am not controlling the system, but instead, the system is controlling me.

Is AI Too Powerful?

At this point, I'm starting to wonder: Has AI come too far? The lift ordering system seems to take on a life of its own at times, with decisions and actions I cannot predict. Is it a bug, or is the system simply learning in ways that I don’t fully understand?

Challenges

The cryptic language and strange behavior of the system make debugging difficult. I’m in the process of figuring out what is causing the inconsistencies, but for now, I’m still trying to get a better handle on this unpredictable automation.
