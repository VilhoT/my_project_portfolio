Liquid Level Sensoring

Problem & Solution

My parents got a new indoor compost/wc system at our cottage, which is great. However, there was a problem: The liquid biowaste (urine) tanks, located in the cellar, would overflow if not emptied often enough. The task of checking the tank level manually was inconvenient, and of course, overflow was a serious problem.

Being there with bright ideas and solutions, I considered various approaches. Should I use load cells to calculate the tank level? What about placing a liquid level sensor inside? Or even use an ultrasound sensor to monitor the liquid level from the outside? But not having all the parts on hand, putting something directly into the tank, and considering cost, I went with a simpler approach.

I ended up using an LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) placed on the opposite side of the almost see-through tank. The idea was to make a LED blink every 2 minutes near the top of the tank. If the liquid level rose too high, the resistance of the LDR would change, triggering another LED to blink rapidly in the house near the toilet door, notifying the users that the tank needed to be emptied.
